Early Life

Outside of Migos, Quavo has been featured on several Billboard Hot 100 top 10 hits, including Post Malone's "Congratulations"—which was certified diamond by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA)—as well as DJ Khaled's "No Brainer" and "I'm the One", the latter of which peaked at number one on the chart.[2][3] In 2018, he released his debut solo album Quavo Huncho, which peaked at number 2 on the Billboard 200.

Career

Quavious Keyate Marshall was born on April 2, 1991, in Athens, Georgia.[4][5][6][7] The three members of Migos grew up together in Gwinnett County, a mostly suburban area 20-30 minutes northeast of Atlanta.[8] Quavo attended Berkmar High School and was the starting quarterback of its football team during the 2009 season, his senior year.[8] Berkmar went in the 2009 season, and Quavo went 19-of-25 for 201 yards and three touchdowns for the first win of the season.[9][10] Quavo previously held the record by completing 28 passes in a game back in 2009 until being surpassed by Taylor Heinicke.[11] Despite finishing the football season for his senior year, Quavo dropped out of Berkmar months before graduation.

Acting

In October 2022, a possible disbandment of Migos became the subject of speculation based on reports that Quavo's ex-girlfriend Saweetie had slept with Offset.[26] Following this, Quavo and Takeoff formed a super duo, Unc & Phew and went on to release a collaborative album titled Only Built for Infinity Links on October 7, 2022 without Offset's input.[27] During an interview with the Big Facts podcast, Quavo stated that he would like to see his and Takeoff's career "as a duo".[28][29]
